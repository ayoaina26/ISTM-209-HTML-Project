Texas A&M University's "The Big Event" is a monumental one-day student-run service project. It started as a way to thank Bryan and College Station locals for their unwavering support of the university and its students. Joe Nussbaum envisioned a time when students would work together, united in purpose, to complete a variety of community service projects. These projects, ranging from revitalizing neighborhood parks to assisting elderly residents with home repairs, frequently include yard work, painting, and other types of essential home maintenance, directly impacting the lives of homeowners who may need a helping hand.

Most of the participants in the event are Texas A&M students who give their time and energy to help the community. The Big Event embodies the fundamental principles of the Aggie Spirit through a strong sense of camaraderie and service. It is founded on a shared desire to express gratitude to the community that so generously supports Texas A&M. Annually, thousands of students participate in the event, making it one of the biggest student-run volunteer initiatives in the country.

Key Terms and Definitions

Aggie Spirit
A deep rooted sense of the Aggie Core Values, often described as an "undefinable, yet awesome force".
Aggieland
A term used colloquially with Texas A&M University.
Aggie Core Values
Excellence, Integrity, Leadership, Loyalty, Respect, and Selfless Service
